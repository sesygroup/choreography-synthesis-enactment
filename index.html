Choreography Synthesis and Enactment at Work

This web page contains the replication package of the paper we submitted to the IEEE Transactions on Services Computing with title Choreography Synthesis and Enactment at Work.

        
        choreography ~ $

Abstract

The Future Internet is increasingly populated by a virtually infinite number of software services. This situation promotes a reuse-based software production through the composition and coordination of existing software services in a way that the specified requirements are met. Service choreographies support a distributed composition approach that is based on the specification of the external interaction of the participant services in terms of flows of peer-to-peer message exchanges, given from a global perspective. When third-party (possibly black-box) services are to be composed, obtaining the distributed coordination logic required to enforce the realizability of the specified choreography is a non-trivial and error prone task. Automatic support is then needed. In this paper, we present a comprehensive software platform offering an integrated development and run-time environment for choreography-based systems, which covers all the development activities, from specification to code synthesis, to automatic deployment, enactment and monitoring on the Cloud. We report the results of an experiment that we conducted with a use case in the in-store marketing and sales domain, and discuss the lesson learned. Results confirm confidence on the approach, and show that the platform can be applied in practical contexts in order to systematically realize correct and efficient choreographies by reusing existing services. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first attempt to bring the adoption of choreographies to the development practices currently adopted by IT companies.

Contributors

Marco Autili

is assistant professor - University of L'Aquila's Department of Information Engineering, Computer Science, and Mathematics. His research focuses on automated synthesis for composing distributed systems, formal specification and checking of temporal properties, context-oriented programming, and resource-oriented analysis of adaptable (mobile) applications. Autili received a PhD in computer science from the University of L'Aquila.

Fabio Kon

is full professor - University of São Paulo, Computer Science. His research focuses on Distributed Systems, Smart Cities, Agile Methods, Open Source, and Entrepreneurship. Prof. Kon is an ACM Distinguished Scientist and a vibraphonist.

Alexander Perucci

is postdoctoral researcher - University of L'Aquila's Department of Information Engineering, Computer Science, and Mathematics. His research focuses on automatic synthesis and composition of software artifacts to build Choreography-Based Service- IoT-Oriented Systems. Perucci received a PhD in computer science from the University of L'Aquila.

Tivoli received a PhD in computer science from the University of L'Aquila.
